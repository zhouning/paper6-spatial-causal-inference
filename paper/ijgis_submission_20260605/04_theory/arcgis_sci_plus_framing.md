# ArcGIS Spatial Causal Inference Plus Framing

Paper 6 should not claim to exceed the full ArcGIS platform. The comparison target is the ArcGIS Spatial Causal Inference / Causal Inference tool class: exposure trimming, covariate balance, exposure-response functions, and target-analysis narratives.

The revised contribution is:

GeoCausal organizes ArcGIS-style causal inference outputs on the county social-capital case, then extends them with open causal-risk auditing. The extension reports spatial residual risk, neighbor-exposure risk when spatial supports are available, variable-role risk, scale-support risk, and reproducible JSON/CSV/Markdown artifacts.

Recommended manuscript claim:

> GeoCausal SCI Plus preserves the operational workflow of ArcGIS Spatial Causal Inference while adding open spatial causal-risk diagnostics that are not visible in the ArcGIS-style output: residual spatial structure, variable-role admissibility, treatment-outcome support mismatch, and target-analysis uncertainty.

Claims to avoid:

- GeoCausal exceeds the ArcGIS platform.
- SG-SCCA is a universal spatial causal identification theory.
- Residual diagnostics prove the absence of spatial confounding.

Recommended evidence:

- Same 0.01--0.99 exposure trimming rule.
- Same or comparable ERF direction and magnitude.
- Explicit target-exposure solver.
- Additional plus report documenting spatial, role, and scale risks.
