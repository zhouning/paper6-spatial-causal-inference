# Cover Letter Draft

Dear Editor,

We are pleased to submit our manuscript, "A Three-Angle Framework for Spatio-Temporal Causal Inference: Integrating Statistical Methods, LLM Reasoning, and World Model Simulation", for consideration in the International Journal of Geographical Information Science.

The manuscript addresses a core GIScience problem: causal inference in geographic settings where spatial confounding, limited experimental control, and multi-scale mechanisms make conventional observational analysis fragile. We propose a three-angle framework that connects statistical causal estimation, LLM-assisted mechanism reasoning, and geospatial world-model simulation through shared spatial-context representations.

The paper makes four contributions:

1. It introduces an integrated framework for geographic causal inference that combines statistical, knowledge-based, and simulation-based reasoning.
2. It develops spatial-context augmentation for causal estimation using geospatial representation features.
3. It defines cross-angle interfaces in which statistical treatment-effect estimates guide mechanism interpretation and calibrate interventional simulation.
4. It validates the framework on synthetic causal scenarios and a real-world urban heat island case study in Chongqing, China.

We believe the manuscript is well aligned with IJGIS because it advances GIScience methodology for spatial causal analysis, geospatial AI, and decision-support-oriented spatial modelling.

The manuscript is original, is not under consideration elsewhere, and all authors have approved the submission. The authors declare no competing interests.
