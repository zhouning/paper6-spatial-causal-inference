# Cover Letter Draft

Dear Editor,

We are pleased to submit our manuscript, "Spatial Context Causal Adjustment: A Diagnostic Workflow for Geographic Observational Studies", for consideration in the International Journal of Geographical Information Science.

The manuscript addresses a core GIScience problem: causal inference in geographic settings where spatial confounding, limited experimental control, and multi-scale measurement make conventional observational analysis fragile. We propose Spatial Context Causal Adjustment (SCCA), a diagnostic workflow that treats spatial context as an auditable adjustment source rather than as an automatically valid control.

The paper makes three contributions:

1. It introduces a reproducible SCCA workflow for constructing, comparing, and diagnosing spatial-context adjustment sets.
2. It provides an evidence-grade protocol covering common support, balance, placebo checks, spatial block bootstrap, residual Moran diagnostics, and spatial-lag sensitivity.
3. It evaluates the workflow through synthetic estimator stress tests, a Chongqing urban heat empirical case, and a county social-capital notebook/GIS reproducibility check.

We believe the manuscript is well aligned with IJGIS because it advances GIScience methodology for spatial causal analysis, spatial diagnostics, and reproducible GIS-facing analytical workflows.

The manuscript is original, is not under consideration elsewhere, and all authors have approved the submission. The authors declare no competing interests.
