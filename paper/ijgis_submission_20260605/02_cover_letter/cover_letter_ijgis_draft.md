Dear Editor,

I am pleased to submit the manuscript "Spatial Context Causal Adjustment: A Diagnostic Workflow for Geographic Observational Studies" for consideration in the International Journal of Geographical Information Science.

The manuscript addresses a core GIScience problem: causal inference in geographic observational studies where treatments, outcomes, and environmental context are spatially structured and often measured on different supports. It proposes Spatial Context Causal Adjustment (SCCA), a diagnostic workflow rather than a new identification theorem.

The paper makes three contributions:

1. It introduces a reproducible SCCA workflow for constructing, comparing, and diagnosing spatial-context adjustment sets.
2. It provides an evidence-grade protocol covering common support, balance, placebo checks, spatial block bootstrap, residual Moran diagnostics, spatial-lag sensitivity, change-of-support diagnostics, and variable-role audits.
3. It evaluates the workflow through synthetic estimator stress tests, a Chongqing urban heat empirical case, a county ArcGIS Causal Inference parity check, and a synthetic positive control.

I believe the manuscript is aligned with IJGIS because it advances GIScience methodology for spatial causal analysis, spatial diagnostics, and reproducible GIS-facing analytical workflows. The empirical claims are deliberately bounded: the Chongqing case is reported at the MODIS outcome support with residual-spatial caution, and the county case is used for ArcGIS algorithmic comparison and GIS reproducibility rather than as independent substantive causal validation.

For peer review, the code, manuscript source, generated result tables, figures, diagnostic reports, synthetic generators, and redistributable public/example data are available in an anonymized reproduction repository: https://anonymous.4open.science/r/spatial-causal-inference-FDFE. Raw Chongqing geospatial inputs and the building-level UHI sample are not publicly redistributed because they include precise geometries or coordinates, floor attributes, and derived environmental variables; the repository provides aggregate-level audit artifacts and structural rerun support for approved local inputs.
